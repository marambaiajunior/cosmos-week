About the project

Cosmos Week as a publication structure, not just a feed.

Cosmos Week is meant to work as an ongoing science news publication with a front page, an archive and visible institutional pages. The point is to give readers both stories and navigational confidence.

Editorial mission

The publication focuses on astronomy, astrophysics, cosmology and frontier science while keeping room for biology, chemistry and Earth science when the material is scientifically substantial and editorially relevant.

The goal is not maximal output. The goal is a readable archive with context and source transparency.

Product structure

  • the homepage serves as the current edition;
  • the archive serves as durable discovery;
  • About and Standards provide institutional grounding;
  • Portuguese and English now use mirrored routes;
  • the menu exposes real sections instead of hiding them in one page.

Coverage logic

Stories are selected for scientific weight, context value and editorial usefulness. A flashy institutional announcement without analytical value does not automatically deserve front-page oxygen.

Automation boundaries

Automation can help with triage and structure. It should not erase the distinction between institutional communication, peer-reviewed literature, preprints and independent science reporting.
